Pink Floyd Major Tour Dates

At the very first ages of the Pink Floyd, the band used to cover R&B songs during the 1965 (Barett Era). During that time Pink Floyd had only four members because Bob Klose had already left after 1965. At that time, the band was pretty much underground. It used to play maximum 50 people at once. In september, 1966, they quickly became the "house band" for the International Times newspaper after playing for its fundraising at All Saint's Church Hall. A very small part of their 1967 show at the UFO club was broadcast in a documentary called It's So Far Out, It's Straight Down which is considered as the first live record of the Pink Floyd. Their first British television appearence was at the The Look of the Week on BBC2 music show on 14 May, 1967. One of the very crucial part of the Pink Floyd shows are their lighting and use of extensive props and pyrotechnic. In their 1994 the Division Bell tour, they used very powerful isotope-splitting copper-vapour lasers which were worth over $120,000 a piece. Pink Floyd's tours did not start until David Gilmour joined their band after Barett had left the band.
