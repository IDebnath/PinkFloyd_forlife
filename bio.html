Syd Barrett

Syd Barrett's real name is Roger Keith "Syd" Barrett. He acquired the nickname "Syd" from his schoolmates after he showed up in a scout site wearing a flat cap. A flat cap used to symbolize the working class. He was born in 6 January, 1946 in Cambridge, Cambridgeshire. He was the fourth of the five children of his father Arthur Max Barrett. Barrett was the co-founder of Pink Floyd and he joined the band when it was named the 'Tea Set' (T-Set). He became known for his English-accented singing and consciousness writing. Syd Barrett recorded several single debut albums: The Piper at the Gates of Dawn (1967), A Saucerful of Secrets (1968) and other unreleased ones. Pink Floyd tributed the songs "Shine On You Crazy Diamond" (1975) and "The Wall" (1979) to Syd Barrett after his retirement from the band in 1972. In 2006, Syd Barrett passed away suffering from pancreatic cancer.

Nick Mason

Nicholas Berkeley Mason, CBE is a founding member of the progressive rockband Pink Floyd. He was born in 27 January, 1944 at Birmingham and brought up at Hampstead. His father, Bill Mason was a documentary film maker. He went to college at Regent Street Polytechnic (at present, the University of Westminister) to study architecture, where he met Rogers Waters, Bob Klose and Richard Wright to form the band "Sigma 6" (earlier Pink Floyd). Nick Mason is the only member of Pink Floyd who got featured in every album as a drummer since its formation. He also had contributions in a lot of Pink Floyd composition writings such as "Echoes", "Time", "Careful with that axe, Eugene" and "One of These Days". He is very passionate about classic race cars and races which is why he has likes to participate in races and owns several classic racing cars like Alfa Romeo 8C, Bugatti Type 35, Ferrari BB LM and so on. Mason was appionted a Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE) for his extra-ordinary contribution in the music industry. Mason is a man of various skills. He is a qualified pilot, a producer and a writer. His net worth amount is $150 million.

Roger Waters

George Roger Waters is an english songwriter, lyricist, bassist, and composer. He is one of the founding members of Pink Floyd. Waters was born in 6 September, 1943 at Great Bookham, Surrey. His father, Eric Fletcher Waters was a devout christian school teacher and a Communist Party member. After Syd Barrett left the Pink Floyd, Waters became their co-lead vocalist and cenceptual leader. Waters staged one of the biggest rock Concerts in the history- The Wall - Live in Berlin - consisting of 450,000 audiences. He is reknown as the highest-grossing toured solo-artist for his Wall Live tour of 2010-2013. His solo works are: Radio K.A.O.S. (1987), Amused to Death (1992), Is This the Life We Really Want? (2017) etc. As a member of Pink Floyd, Roger Waters occupied US Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1996) and UK Music Hall of Fame (2005). Waters has net worth of $310 million.

Richard Wright

Richard William Wright was a keyboardist, co-founder and one of the vocalists of Pink Floyd. He was born on 28 July, 1943 in Hatch End, Middlesex. He learned guitar, trombone, trumpet and piano at the age of 12 all by himself. Wright's mother asked him to focus on piano, as a result, he started taking piano lessons. Richard Wright met Roger Waters and Nick Mason while studying architecture in University of Westminister. In 1981, Richard Wright left the band after touring The Wall. He rejoined the band for A Momentary Lapse of Reason (1987) as a session player and The Division Bell (1994) as a full time. Wright's distinctive keyboard playing was a very crucial part of the Pink Floyd's sound. In 1984, Richard retired from the music business and moved to Greece because he had a fascination about greek islands and sailing.

David Gilmour

David Jon Gilmour is an english musician who joined Pink Floyd in 1968 just before Syd Barrett left the band. He was born on 6 March, 1946 in Cambridge, England. His father, Douglas Gilmour was a senior lecturer at the University of Cambridge and his mother, Sylvia was a film editor of BBC. His parents encouraged his interest in music. David met his other band memebers in Perse School on Hills Road. In 1962, he started his career in the music industry by joining a rock blue band called "Jokers Wild". In 1967, Nick Mason invited David Gilmour to cover for Syd Barrett and Gilmour accepted the offer. In his solo album About Face, he expressed his fellings about the murder of John Lennon, his relationship with Waters and so many other diversed topics. One of the biggest hits of Pink Floyd, "Comfortably Numb" came out of David Gilmour's solo album David Gilmour. All four of his solo albums charted in top 40 in the United States. His achievements throughout his carrer includes: US Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1996), Commander of the Order of the British Empire (2003), UK Music Hall of Fame (2005), and Q Awards (2008). He was ranked 14th amongs the greatest guitarists of all time by Rolling Stones Magazine in 2011. David Gilmour has a net worth of $180 million.

Bob Klose

Rado Robert Garcia Klose is an English musician, photographer and printmaker. He was born in Cambridge on 1945. Klose completed high school and joined the architecture school in the same university as the other band members of Pink Floyd. Bob Klose was in the band before the Pink Floyd was established and left before it formatted. He left the band because he was more serious about his studies than the band, he wanted to focus more in his academic career. Later in 1960's he abandoned his studies and started photography parmanently. Richard Wright quoted Bob as the best guitarist they had ever had and also stated that he had some exam problems; as a result he had to leave the band.
